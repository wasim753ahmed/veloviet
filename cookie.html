1. What are cookies?

A cookie is a small text file stored on your device to help websites and mobile applications remember things about you. Cookies can be session-based, existing only while your browser is open, or persistent, lasting until deleted or expired. To learn more about cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.

Other technologies, such as pixel tags and social media features, may also function as tracking tools. For example, social media features like the Facebook "like" button may collect your IP address and set cookies to operate correctly.

2. How We Use Cookies

The cookies we use can be classified as follows:

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Enable site features, like navigation and form submission.
  • Security cookies: Help identify and prevent security risks.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: Collect data to improve website performance.
  • Functional and preference cookies: Remember choices you make, such as language settings.
  • Targeting and advertising cookies: Deliver relevant ads based on your website usage.
